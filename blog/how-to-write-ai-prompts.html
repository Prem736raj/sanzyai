How to Write AI Prompts That Actually Work

📅 April 2026 ✍️ SanzyAI Team 📂 Tutorial / AI

Most people get disappointing results from AI because they prompt like they're Googling: short, vague, and context-free. Good prompting is a skill, and once you learn the framework, every AI tool — ChatGPT, Claude, Midjourney, Gemini — gives dramatically better results.

This guide teaches you the ROLE-TASK-FORMAT framework that professional prompt engineers use.

The Problem: Why Most Prompts Fail

❌ Bad Prompt
Write me a marketing plan
✅ Good Prompt
Act as a senior digital marketing strategist with 10 years of experience in SaaS. Create a 90-day marketing plan for a B2B project management tool targeting remote teams of 10-50 people. Include: channel strategy, content calendar, budget allocation for $5K/month, KPIs, and a weekly reporting template. Format as a structured document with clear sections and action items.

The difference? Context, specificity, and structure. The good prompt tells the AI exactly who to be, what to do, and how to format the output.

The ROLE-TASK-FORMAT Framework

🎭 R — Role

Tell the AI who it is. "Act as a..." gives the AI a perspective and expertise level.

Examples: "Act as a senior copywriter", "You are a data scientist", "Behave like a UX researcher"

📋 T — Task

Be specific about what you want. Include context, constraints, and the exact deliverable.

Bad: "Write an email" → Good: "Write a follow-up email to a prospect who attended our webinar but didn't book a demo"

📐 F — Format

Tell the AI exactly how to structure the output. Tables, bullet points, specific length, tone.

Examples: "Format as a table", "Keep under 200 words", "Use a professional but friendly tone"

5 Power Techniques

1. Chain Prompting

Break complex tasks into steps. Use the output of prompt 1 as input for prompt 2.

Step 1: "List 10 blog post ideas about AI productivity" Step 2: "Take idea #3 and create a detailed outline with H2/H3 structure" Step 3: "Write the introduction section using the outline above"

2. Few-Shot Examples

Show the AI examples of what you want before asking for the output.

Write product descriptions in this style: Example 1: "The CloudPen writes itself. Voice-to-text in 12 languages, 8-hour battery, weighs less than your keys." Example 2: "SleepPod. White noise meets smart alarm. Learns your sleep cycle. Wakes you in your lightest phase." Now write one for: A portable espresso maker that uses USB-C charging

3. Constraint Setting

Tell the AI what NOT to do. Constraints improve quality.

Write a landing page headline for a time tracking app. Constraints: - Maximum 8 words - No clichés like "revolutionize" or "game-changing" - Must include a specific benefit - Target audience: freelancers

4. Output Formatting Control

Be extremely specific about format to get consistent, usable results.

Respond in this exact JSON format: { "headline": "...", "subheadline": "...", "cta_button": "...", "social_proof": "..." }

5. Iterative Refinement

Follow up with improvement prompts instead of starting over.

Follow-up prompts: → "Make this more concise" → "Add more specific examples" → "Rewrite in a conversational tone" → "What's missing from this plan?"
